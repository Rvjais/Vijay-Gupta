About Dr. Vikas Gupta

Dr. Vikas Gupta is one of India’s leading Senior Consultant Neurosurgeons at Kailash Deepak Multispecialty Hospital, Delhi NCR. With over 30 years of experience in brain and spine surgery, he is a well-known figure in the treatment of complex brain tumor microsurgery, neurovascular surgery, minimally invasive spine surgery, and comprehensive stroke treatment. Among the few neurosurgeons in India dual-trained in both cerebrovascular surgery and interventional neuroradiology, Dr. Gupta provides exceptional care for aneurysms, AVMs, skull base tumors, spine disorders, and emergency stroke cases. With more than 3,000 successful neurosurgical procedures and thousands of patients treated across Delhi-NCR, he is recognized as a trusted and experienced neurosurgeon in Noida for endovascular, brain tumor, and spine surgery.

My Professional Journey

I am Dr. Vikas Gupta, currently serving as a Senior Consultant Neurosurgeon at Kailash Deepak Multispecialty Hospital, Delhi NCR, where we focus on advanced neurosurgery and comprehensive stroke care. Over the past 30 years, I have had the privilege of building and leading expert teams for both elective and emergency neurosurgeries, with a special focus on acute stroke interventions, aneurysm coiling, AVM embolization, and other neurovascular procedures.

Throughout my career, I have undertaken complex, high-risk surgeries, including the successful separation of esophageal-conjoined twins from Nigeria, a rare and challenging procedure in the private healthcare sector. As a pioneer in neurovascular and endovascular neurosurgery in India, I have trained numerous neurosurgery fellows, led neurology and stroke outreach programs across North India, and contributed to the development of advanced acute stroke services in the Delhi-NCR region. I am committed to precision, innovation, and ethical patient care, and I dedicate myself to treating brain, spine, and vascular neurological disorders with the highest standards of expertise and compassion.

Areas of Expertise

Craniotomy Surgery

Involves temporarily removing a section of the skull to access the brain for treating tumours, bleeding, injuries, or other neurological conditions safely.

Microvascular Decompression

A precise neurosurgical procedure that relieves nerve pain by gently separating compressed cranial nerves from nearby blood vessels, treating conditions like trigeminal neuralgia effectively.

Deep Brain Stimulation (DBS)

Surgical treatment that uses implanted electrodes to deliver controlled electrical signals to specific brain areas, helping manage movement disorders and neurological symptoms safely.

Carotid Artery Stenting

A minimally invasive procedure that places a small mesh tube in a narrowed carotid artery to restore blood flow and reduce the risk of stroke.

Minimally Invasive Brain Tumour Removal

Uses advanced imaging and keyhole techniques to safely remove tumours with minimal damage, less pain, quicker recovery, and improved outcomes.
