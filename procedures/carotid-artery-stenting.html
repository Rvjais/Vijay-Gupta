Carotid Artery Stenting in Delhi

Carotid stenting is a minimally invasive procedure that opens clogged arteries to restore blood flow to the brain. They’re often performed to treat or prevent strokes.

The carotid arteries are located on each side of your neck. These are the main arteries supplying blood to your brain. They can be clogged with fatty deposits (plaque) that slow or block blood flow to the brain, which can lead to a stroke.

The procedure involves temporarily inserting and inflating a tiny balloon into the clogged artery to widen the area so that blood can flow freely to your brain.

According to global statistics, carotid artery stenting is a widely performed procedure, with thousands of cases performed annually. Around 1,00,000 carotid artery stenting procedures are conducted each year. The procedure has also gained popularity in Europe, Asia, and other regions as a viable treatment option for carotid artery disease. 

While it’s a commonly performed intervention, many wonder about its safety and effectiveness. In this blog, we’ll explore the safety aspects of carotid artery stenting, exploring its benefits and risks.

What is Carotid Artery Disease?

Carotid artery disease refers to the narrowing or blockage of the carotid arteries, the major blood vessels on each side of the neck that supply oxygen-rich blood to the brain. This condition typically results from plaque buildup, consisting of cholesterol, fat, and other substances, along the inner walls of the arteries. 

As the arteries become narrowed or blocked, blood flow to the brain is restricted, increasing the risk of stroke. Carotid artery disease often develops gradually and may not cause symptoms until it becomes severe, making early detection and management essential for stroke prevention.

What is the Role of Carotid Artery Stenting?

Carotid artery stenting is crucial in treating carotid artery disease by restoring proper blood flow to the brain. This minimally invasive procedure involves inserting a small mesh tube called a stent into the narrowed or blocked carotid artery to widen it and improve blood flow. 

By opening up the artery, carotid artery stenting reduces the risk of stroke and other complications associated with reduced blood flow to the brain. It is often recommended for patients who are at high risk for complications from traditional surgery or those who prefer a less invasive treatment option.

FAQs

  1. Is carotid artery stenting painful?

Carotid artery stenting is typically not painful, as it is performed under anesthesia, and patients may experience minimal discomfort afterward.

  1. How long does a carotid artery stenting procedure take?

The procedure usually takes about 1 to 2 hours, including preparation time.

  1. What are the potential complications of carotid artery stenting?

Complications may include stroke, bleeding, infection, or allergic reactions, but these are relatively rare.

  1. How soon can I resume normal activities after carotid artery stenting?

Depending on individual recovery, patients can resume normal activities within a few days to a week after the procedure.

  1. Is carotid artery stenting covered by insurance?

In many cases, carotid artery stenting is covered by insurance, but it’s essential to check with your insurance provider for specific coverage details.
