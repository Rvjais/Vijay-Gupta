Carpal Tunnel Syndrome Treatment in Delhi: Symptoms, Causes & Expert Care

Carpal Tunnel Syndrome (CTS) is a prevalent condition worldwide, with varying rates in different regions. According to a study published in the Journal of Orthopaedic Surgery and Research, the prevalence of CTS among the general population ranges from 1% to 5%.

However, certain occupational groups, such as office workers, assembly line workers, and musicians, have a higher risk, with prevalence rates as high as 14% to 18%.

This is because, for office workers, long hours of sitting and improper workstation setup can lead to issues like back pain and carpal tunnel syndrome. Assembly line workers often perform repetitive motions, leading to strains and sprains. Musicians, especially those playing instruments requiring repetitive movements or holding heavy instruments, are prone to overuse injuries. The high prevalence rates in these groups highlight the importance of ergonomics in preventing musculoskeletal disorders. Implementing ergonomic practices can significantly reduce your risk of developing CTS.

What is Carpal Tunnel Syndrome?

Carpal Tunnel Syndrome (CTS) is a common condition that affects millions of people worldwide, especially those who perform repetitive hand and wrist movements. It occurs when the median nerve runs from the forearm into the palm and becomes compressed or squeezed at the wrist. This can cause pain, numbness, tingling, and weakness in the hand and arm.

What is the Role of Ergonomics in Preventing Carpal Tunnel Syndrome?

Ergonomics is crucial in preventing CTS by optimizing the interaction between people, equipment, and their environment. Individuals can reduce the strain on their hands and wrists by creating ergonomic workspaces and habits, lowering their risk of developing CTS.

Dr. Vikas explains, “Ergonomics focuses on creating a workspace that minimizes strain on your body. For Carpal Tunnel Syndrome, this means ensuring your wrists are properly supported and that you’re not placing undue pressure on them while working. Simple adjustments, like using an ergonomic keyboard and mouse, maintaining good posture, and taking regular breaks, can significantly prevent CTS.”

What Are the Best Ergonomic Practices to Prevent this Syndrome?

The optimal ergonomic practices to prevent this syndrome include:

  • Maintaining a Proper Posture: Sit or stand with your back straight and shoulders relaxed. Avoid slouching or hunching over your desk.
  • Using Proper Keyboard and Mouse Technique: Keep your wrists straight and your hands at or slightly below the level of your elbows. Use a wrist rest to support your wrists while typing or using the mouse.
  • Taking Frequent Breaks: Avoid prolonged periods of repetitive hand movements. Take short breaks every 20-30 minutes to stretch and rest your hands and wrists.
  • Using Ergonomic Tools: Invest in ergonomic keyboards, mice, and other tools designed to reduce strain on your hands and wrists.
  • Adjusting the Workspace: Ensure that your workstation is set up correctly. Your monitor should be at eye level, and your keyboard and mouse should be within easy reach.
  • Practicing a Proper Typing Technique: Use light keystrokes and avoid pounding on the keyboard—type with your fingers and not your wrists.
  • Stretching Regularly: Perform wrist stretches and exercises to help prevent stiffness and improve flexibility.
  • Managing Weight: Excess weight can increase pressure on your wrists and hands, so maintaining a healthy weight can help prevent CTS.
  • Using Proper Lifting Techniques: When lifting heavy objects, use your whole hand to grip, rather than just your fingers, to reduce strain on your wrists.
  • Considering Ergonomic Accessories: Consider using ergonomic accessories such as wrist braces or splints to support your wrists during activities that may strain them.

What is the Cost of Treating Carpal Tunnel Syndrome in India?

The cost of treating Carpal Tunnel Syndrome (CTS) in India can vary depending on the severity of the condition and the treatment approach.

  • Non-surgical treatments such as wrist splints, medications, and physiotherapy may cost between ₹500 to ₹3000 INR per session, and a typical course of treatment may range from ₹3000 to ₹20,000 INR.
  • Surgical treatment for CTS, such as endoscopic carpal tunnel release surgery, may cost between ₹25,000 to ₹1,50,000 INR, depending on the hospital and surgeon’s fees.

You must consult a healthcare provider for an accurate estimate based on your needs.

Carpal Tunnel Syndrome can be a debilitating condition, but it is preventable with the correct ergonomic practices. You can significantly reduce your risk of developing CTS by maintaining proper posture, using ergonomic tools, taking breaks, and practicing proper typing techniques. Prevention is critical, so start implementing these ergonomic practices today to protect your hands and wrists for years.
