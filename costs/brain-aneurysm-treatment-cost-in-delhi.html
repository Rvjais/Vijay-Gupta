Understanding Brain Aneurysm Treatment Cost in Delhi

The cost of brain aneurysm treatment in Delhi varies based on several medical factors. Whilst we provide indicative cost ranges to help you plan, please understand that exact costs can only be determined after clinical evaluation and review of your scans, as recommended by ethical medical guidelines.

Average Cost Range

Brain aneurysm treatment costs in Delhi generally range from ₹1,50,000 to ₹20,00,000.

Important: These are indicative estimates only. Your treatment team will provide personalised cost estimates after thorough evaluation.

Detailed Cost Breakdown for Brain Aneurysm Treatment

A transparent overview of treatment-related expenses, helping families understand estimated costs at each stage of brain aneurysm care and plan treatment with clarity and confidence.

1. Surgical Treatment Costs

Procedure Type Approximate Cost Range (INR)
Endovascular coiling (standard) ₹3,00,000 – ₹8,00,000
Complex coiling with stent/flow diverter ₹8,00,000 – ₹15,00,000
Microsurgical clipping (standard) ₹2,50,000 – ₹6,00,000
Complex/giant aneurysm surgery ₹6,00,000 – ₹12,00,000
Ruptured aneurysm (emergency) ₹5,00,000 – ₹20,00,000

What influences treatment costs:

  • Aneurysm characteristics: Size, location, shape, and neck width determine treatment complexity
  • Treatment approach: Endovascular (coiling) vs microsurgical (clipping)
  • Aneurysm status: Unruptured aneurysms cost less than ruptured ones, requiring emergency intervention
  • Technology used: Flow diverters, stent-assisted coiling, intraoperative angiography, and advanced neuronavigation
  • Surgeon expertise: Experience in complex neurovascular procedures
  • Operating time: Longer procedures require extended anaesthesia and theatre resources
  • Hospital infrastructure: Advanced ICU facilities, neurointensive care units, and monitoring equipment

When Should You See a Neurosurgeon?

For Unruptured Aneurysms (Often Found Incidentally)

Seek consultation if:

  • An aneurysm has been discovered on imaging for other reasons
  • You have a family history of aneurysms (two or more close relatives)
  • You have conditions associated with aneurysms (polycystic kidney disease, certain connective tissue disorders)
  • You’re concerned about your risk factors

For Ruptured Aneurysms (Medical Emergency)

Seek immediate emergency care if you experience:

  • Sudden, severe headache: Often described as “the worst headache of my life” or “thunderclap headache.”
  • Sudden neck pain or stiffness: Particularly with headache
  • Nausea and vomiting: Especially with severe headache
  • Loss of consciousness: Even brief
  • Seizures: New-onset seizure
  • Vision problems: Sudden double vision or vision loss
  • Confusion or altered mental status
  • Sensitivity to light: Photophobia
  • Drooping eyelid or dilated pupil

Critical: A ruptured aneurysm is a life-threatening emergency. Call emergency services immediately if you experience these symptoms.

Early diagnosis and treatment of unruptured aneurysms often lead to better outcomes, less complex procedures, shorter recovery, and lower overall costs.
