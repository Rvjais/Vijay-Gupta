Understanding Brain Tumour Surgery Cost in Delhi

The cost of brain tumour surgery in Delhi varies based on several medical factors. While we provide indicative cost ranges to help you plan, please understand that exact costs can only be determined after clinical evaluation and review of your scans, as recommended by ethical medical guidelines.

Average Cost Range

Brain tumour surgery costs in Delhi generally range from ₹1,00,000 to ₹25,00,000.

Important: These are indicative estimates only. Your treatment team will provide personalised cost estimates after thorough evaluation.

Detailed Cost Breakdown for Brain Tumour Treatment

A transparent overview of treatment-related expenses, helping families understand estimated costs at each stage of brain tumour care and plan treatment with clarity and confidence.

1. Surgical Treatment Costs

Procedure Type Approximate Cost Range (INR)
Standard brain tumour surgery ₹1,00,000 – ₹5,00,000
Complex/skull base surgery ₹5,00,000 – ₹12,00,000

What influences surgical costs:

  • Tumour location: Deep-seated or skull base tumours require more complex microsurgical techniques
  • Surgical approach: Minimally invasive endoscopic surgery vs traditional craniotomy
  • Technology used: Intraoperative MRI, neuronavigation, fluorescence-guided surgery
  • Surgeon expertise: Experience and specialisation in complex cases
  • Operating time: Longer procedures require extended anaesthesia and theatre resources
  • Hospital infrastructure: Advanced ICU facilities and monitoring equipment

When Should You See a Neurosurgeon?

Seek immediate medical evaluation if you experience:

  • Persistent headaches: Especially if worsening, different from usual headaches, or worse in the morning
  • Seizures: New-onset seizures in adults always warrant investigation
  • Vision problems: Blurred vision, double vision, or visual field loss
  • Speech difficulties: Trouble finding words or understanding speech
  • Weakness: Progressive weakness in the arm, leg, or face
  • Balance problems: Unsteadiness, frequent falls, or coordination difficulties
  • Memory or personality changes: Confusion, behavioural changes, or cognitive decline
  • Nausea and vomiting: Particularly if associated with headache and worse in the morning

Early diagnosis often leads to better outcomes, less complex surgery, and lower overall costs.
