When should I consult a neurosurgeon? expand_more

You should see a neurosurgeon if you have persistent headaches, dizziness, weakness, vision changes, seizures, severe back or neck pain, numbness, sudden stroke-like symptoms, or if you’ve been advised to undergo imaging for a suspected brain or spine issue.

What symptoms indicate a possible brain or vascular problem? expand_more

Warning signs include: sudden severe headache, one-sided weakness, slurred speech, loss of consciousness, seizures, double vision, imbalance, or sudden numbness. These may require urgent specialist evaluation.

How do I know if I need treatment for a brain aneurysm or AVM? expand_more

If your scan shows an aneurysm or AVM, Dr. Gupta will assess its size, shape, and risk factors to suggest whether monitoring, endovascular treatment, or surgery is required.

What should I do if someone shows signs of stroke? expand_more

Call emergency services and reach the nearest stroke-ready hospital immediately. Every minute matters. Dr. Gupta and his team offer emergency stroke treatment, including clot removal and advanced neuro-intervention.

Is endovascular (minimally invasive) treatment safer than open surgery? expand_more

For many conditions, yes. Endovascular treatment is performed using small catheters, resulting in less pain, faster recovery, and a shorter hospital stay. Dr. Gupta offers both options and recommends the safest one for each case.

How long will I need to stay in the hospital after brain or spine surgery? expand_more

Hospital stay may range from 2-7 days, depending on the complexity of the procedure and your recovery progress. Most minimally invasive procedures allow quicker discharge.

What preparations are required before surgery? expand_more

You may need routine blood tests, imaging, an ECG, and medical clearance. Dr. Gupta’s team will explain fasting instructions, medicines to stop, and everything required for a smooth procedure.

Will I feel pain after brain or spine surgery? expand_more

Some discomfort is normal, but pain is well-managed with medication. Dr. Gupta uses minimally invasive techniques whenever possible to reduce pain and speed up recovery.

What risks are involved in neurosurgery? expand_more

All procedures have risks, but choosing an experienced neurosurgeon significantly reduces them. Dr. Gupta discusses every risk transparently so you can make an informed decision.
